Understanding the Four Features:

  • Credit Usage:: This measures how much of the available credit a customer is using. High credit usage (e.g., using 80% or more of available credit) credit is generally considered risky as it suggests financial strain or poor management of credit. Low credit usage (e.g., using less than 30% of available credit) of is usually seen as responsible credit utilization and indicates lower risk.
  • Payment History: This is the record of a customer's payment history on debts, including loans and credit cards. A consistent record of timely payments indicates a low-risk customer. In contrast, late payments, defaults, or bankruptcies can signal higher risk. Payment history is often considered the most critical factor in credit risk assessment.
  • Debt: This feature looks at the total amount of money a customer owes, including credit card balances, loans, and other obligations. High debt levels, particularly when compared to income, suggest a higher risk of default. Lower debt levels, especially when paired with high income, are generally safer.
  • Income: A customer's income provides context for their ability to repay debts. Higher incomes typically correlate with lower risk, as individuals are more likely to have sufficient resources to meet their obligations. Low income, particularly when paired with high debt, can indicate a high-risk scenario.
